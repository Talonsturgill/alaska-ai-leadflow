# Alaska Market Notes

Context so every dossier reads like it was written by someone who knows the state,
not a stranger who searched it. Use it to frame and to sound credible. Verify the
specifics for each lead, these are the lay of the land, not claims to paste.

## The labor frame (our sharpest wedge)
Do not lead with "worker shortage," Alaska is mid-tier on that index. The real,
defensible frame is cost plus scarcity of qualified applicants plus 13 straight
years of net out-migration plus extreme seasonality. Roughly a quarter of the
workforce is nonresident. The line that lands, AI is how an Alaska business does
more with the workers it can actually get.

## Know Alaska, never fake being there
Alaskans, and especially Alaska Native corporations, prefer a partner who genuinely
understands their world, the season, freeze-up, the flights, the market. That
understanding is our edge, and we earn it in the research, not by claiming a location
we do not have. We are Alaska AI, focused on this market, but we are NOT physically
in-state, so never write "we are here", "buy local from us", "in-state", or anything
that implies a local storefront. Honesty is the whole brand. Show we know Alaska by
being specific and right about their business, that is the only local proof we offer.

## Where the money and pain concentrate
- Tourism and visitor industry. Seasonal labor crunch, booking and front-desk
  load, a four-month window. Thousands of operators. Voice agents and content pay
  fast.
- Healthcare. Largest private industry. Documentation, scheduling, intake, and
  prior-auth are the highest-ROI uses anywhere. Target independents, the big
  systems are enterprise.
- Alaska Native corporations. Among the largest companies in the state, with
  federal-contracting DNA and 8(a) sole-source volume. Proposal and compliance
  drafting is a killer use case. Best ability to pay, deep buy-local affinity.
- Paperwork generally. Permits, RFPs, solicitations, compliance filings. The back
  office is where the verified ROI lives.

## What we build (map every lead to one of these)
Voice and front-desk agents, RAG assistants over their own files, workflow
automation, the paperwork and proposal engine, digital employees, and at the
ceiling an agentic operating system that is a working model of the business. Every
build ends with a model they own, so their costs fall over time instead of
climbing.

## The honest stat spine (verify before quoting in any message)
Most AI spend underperforms, and the winners redesign the workflow rather than buy
a tool. Small businesses have adopted AI broadly but very few have it wired into
operations. Use these to frame, not to bluff. If a number goes into an outreach,
it carries a real source.
