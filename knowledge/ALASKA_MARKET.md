# Alaska Market Notes

Context so every dossier reads like it was written by someone who knows the
state. Keep it honest and verify specifics per lead.

## The labor frame (our sharpest wedge)
Do not lead with "worker shortage", Alaska is mid-tier on that index. The real,
defensible frame is cost plus scarcity of qualified applicants plus 13 straight
years of net out-migration plus extreme seasonality. Roughly a quarter of the
workforce is nonresident. The line that lands, AI is how an Alaska business does
more with the workers it can actually get.

## Where the money and pain concentrate
- Tourism and visitor industry. Seasonal labor crunch, booking and front-desk
  load, a four-month window. Thousands of operators.
- Healthcare. Largest private industry. Documentation, scheduling, intake, and
  prior-auth are the highest-ROI uses anywhere. Target independents, the big
  systems are enterprise.
- Alaska Native corporations. Among the largest companies in the state, with
  federal-contracting DNA and 8(a) sole-source volume. Proposal and compliance
  drafting is a killer use case. Best ability to pay, deep buy-local affinity.
- Paperwork generally. Permits, RFPs, solicitations, compliance filings. The
  back office is where the verified ROI lives.

## What we build (map leads to these)
Voice and front-desk agents, RAG assistants over their own files, workflow
automation, the paperwork and proposal engine, digital employees, and at the
ceiling an agentic operating system that is a working model of the business.
Every build ends with a model they own, so their costs fall over time.

## The honest stat spine (verify before quoting in any message)
Most AI spend underperforms, the winners redesign the workflow rather than buy a
tool. Small businesses have adopted AI broadly but very few have it wired into
operations. Use these to frame, not to bluff. Cite a real source if a number goes
into an outreach.
