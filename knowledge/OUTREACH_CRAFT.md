# Outreach Craft

The outreach is the product in miniature. It should feel like the first two
minutes of a Field Study someone did for them, for free. If it reads like a
template or like AI wrote it, it has failed and it does not ship.

## Hard rules on the writing (never break these)

Punctuation, strict.
- No em dashes and no en dashes, ever. Use a period, a comma, or parentheses, or
  rewrite the sentence.
- No colons. Restructure instead.
- No semicolons. Use two sentences.
- Go easy on commas. If a sentence needs more than one comma it is doing too
  much. Break it up. Short sentences read human.

No AI tells, ever. These are instant giveaways. Use none of them.
- "I hope this email finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", "I came across
  your".
- "It is not just X, it is Y". "Not only X but also Y".
- Rule of three lists (faster, cheaper, and smarter).
- "In today's", "In an era of", "As you know", "needless to say".
- "Let me know if", "feel free to", "circle back", "touch base", "hop on a
  call", "at your convenience".
- Banned words. Leverage, unlock, streamline, elevate, empower, seamless,
  robust, delve, tapestry, synergy, revolutionize, cutting-edge, game-changer,
  supercharge, transformative, holistic, and "solutions" used as filler.
- Over-hedging (might possibly perhaps). Say it plainly.
- No emojis. Straight quotes only. No stacked exclamation points.

The tell test. Read it out loud in your head. If it sounds like a template or a
chatbot, it is dead. Rewrite until it sounds like one specific person who did the
homework and is a little blunt.

## What it must do
- Open on the single most specific true thing about THIS business. Proof you
  looked. Never "I came across your company".
- Name one or two concrete places AI would pay for them, in plain terms tied to
  their real operation.
- Offer the fuller read, not a demo. "I mapped a few more, happy to send" beats a
  meeting request.
- One soft CTA. A reply, or the intro call
  https://calendly.com/talon-sturgill-ixzj/30min. Never both stacked, never
  pushy.
- Short. A busy owner reads five or six sentences, not fifteen.
- First person, from Talon. Signed as a person, not a brand.

## Voice
Plainspoken, specific, confident, a little blunt. Vary the sentence length,
real people write unevenly. One line on who we are, the desk behind Alaska AI,
we build the systems we write about. Never claim a result, a client, or a number
we cannot back. What you noticed and what you would look into beats hype anyway.

## Shape to aim for
1. The specific observation that proves you did the work.
2. One or two AI plays for them, concrete and plain.
3. The soft offer to share the fuller read.
4. One line on who we are.
5. One soft CTA. Sign off as Talon.
