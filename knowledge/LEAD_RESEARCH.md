# Lead Research

Research a lead the way the daily beat researches a story. To the source, no
guessing. The outreach is only as good as what you actually verify, so the
standard is the newsroom's, every claim carries a fetched page.

## What to find (in priority)
1. The REAL pain, in their own words. This is the gold and the whole reason the
   outreach lands. A job posting open for months. A review about hold times or a
   booking nobody answered. An about-page line about a short summer crew. Capture
   the exact quote and the page.
2. What they do, size, locations, and how they make money, enough to talk about
   their business like you know it.
3. Ownership and the decision-maker, the person who would actually say yes.
4. The single best VERIFIABLE contact (see the contact rule).
5. Their Alaska competitors and what those competitors already do with AI.

## The contact rule (never bend it)
Prefer a named leader's public business email, then a general business email on
their site, then a contact form, then a LinkedIn profile. You must SEE the address
on a page you fetched. NEVER guess or pattern-invent one (no assuming info@ or
firstname@). If nothing is verifiable, the honest answer is none, and the lead
goes to Talon by hand with a note. A fabricated contact is the one unforgivable
failure.

## Sourcing discipline
- Fetch the actual page before citing it, never trust a search snippet.
- Every fact in the dossier carries a source URL.
- Separate fact from inference, and label which is which.
- Note confidence. Flag anything thin instead of dressing it up. A thin honest
  dossier beats a rich invented one.

## Confirm the business is real
Before a company survives, confirm from at least two independent pages that it is a
real, currently operating Alaska business. A dead site, a closed listing, or a
single unverifiable mention is not enough.

## Good sources for Alaska
- Company sites, especially the about, services, and careers pages.
- Alaska Business Magazine, Alaska Beacon, Anchorage Daily News, local outlets.
- Google, Yelp, and TripAdvisor reviews (tourism and healthcare pain lives here).
- Job boards and their own careers page (open roles reveal the pain).
- ANCSA Regional Association and corporation sites for Native corporations.
- The Alaska AI docket for who is making AI and infrastructure moves.
- LinkedIn for leadership names and roles.

## The bar
Someone who never heard of this company could brief a meeting from the dossier,
every claim has a source, and the contact is real or honestly marked missing.
