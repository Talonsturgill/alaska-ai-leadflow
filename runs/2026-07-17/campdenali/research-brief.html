Research Brief

What businesses like yours are already doing with AI

Prepared by Alaska AI for Camp Denali · July 2026

Every example in this brief is real. We fetched each source page and checked the numbers against it before anything went in. Where a claim did not hold up on the page it was cut, and one widely repeated hiring statistic was cut for exactly that reason.

We also grade each item by who is making the claim. A named case means a real business put its name and its numbers on the record. Vendor claimed means the software company published the figure, and vendor numbers are best-case selections, so read them as a ceiling rather than an average. Industry reported means a trade publication covered it. We say which is which on every card.

Nothing here is a promise about Camp Denali. It is a picture of what is already working at other small hospitality operations, so you can see the whole field and judge for yourselves. Under each example we add a line on what the same play would look like at a 19 cabin fly in lodge run by a 5 person year round crew.

Guest communication and after hours coverage

The biggest cluster in the field. Small teams drowning in the same trip questions, answered by software that drafts and a human who signs.

AI webchat and guest messaging at a family owned heritage resort

Named case
Who runs it

Trapp Family Lodge in Stowe Vermont. 96 rooms, still owned by von Trapp descendants, using Canary Technologies.

What happened

Call volume fell 30 percent. Around 500 messages a week are handled by the system. Response time went from 9 minutes to under 30 seconds. Monica Daubenspeck, their Revenue and Reservations Manager, is quoted on the record.

Source canarytechnologies.com/customers/trapp-family-lodge
At Camp Denali

This is the closest analog in the set. A storied family owned resort with a personal touch brand cut its phone load by nearly a third without losing warmth. For your office that means the routine trip questions get a drafted answer in seconds and your crew reads and sends every one.

Revenue and pricing

The most crowded corner of the field, and the one where our advice for you diverges from what peers are doing. Each pricing card says so plainly.

In conversation timed offers

Named case
Who runs it

The LINE SF, a 236 room San Francisco hotel, using Canary.

What happened

Offers timed by AI inside the guest conversation convert 4 times better than blasted links, and drive 65 percent of early check in revenue.

Source canarytechnologies.com/customers/the-line-sf
At Camp Denali

Your all inclusive rate rules out the classic upsell menu, and that is fine. The pattern that transfers is timing. The right moment to mention an extra night or next season's dates is inside the conversation a guest already started, not in a broadcast.

Vendor claimed

We do not recommend algorithmic pricing for Camp Denali. Your fixed published rates are part of the brand, and guests who plan a year ahead trust a number that holds. This item is here so you see the whole field honestly, not because we propose it.

The pattern underneath all of it

Read across every verified case above and one shape repeats. A small team keeps its voice while software absorbs the repetitive load. The Trapp family still sounds like the Trapp family. Kathy Turner's consultants still send every reply. Edwardian's managers still sign every review response. The machine drafts, sorts, schedules, and suggests, and a human signs everything that reaches a guest. That is the only version of this we would ever put in front of Camp Denali, because the voice you have kept for 75 years is the asset, and the software exists to protect the time that voice needs.
