The After-Hours Backstop

Prepared by Alaska AI for Camp Denali. July 2026.
What saying yes sets in motion, in order, with the gate where you stay in control at every step.

This is the plan for one build. A phone agent that sits quietly behind your existing 907-683-2290 line and wakes only when no person catches the call, the closed hours, the noon to one lunch closure, the unanswered overflow, and the carrier failures your own contact page warns about. It answers common questions from a fact base your family approves word for word, it says plainly that it is the after-hours line, it takes the caller's name, phone, email, dates, party size, and three, four, or seven night preference, and it leaves one clean morning note per call on your desk before the office opens. The sequencing is built so you carry no risk you did not choose. Each phase is short and honest, each ends at a gate that is your off-ramp, and the worst case of the finished product is the phone you have today, because pulling the forwarding rules restores it in minutes. You own the whole system at the end.

3 Wire the line 3 business days to 2 weeks

We route only the calls no human caught to the backstop, through conditional forwarding behind your existing 907-683-2290 number, without changing the published number and without ever touching a call a person answers.

Deliverable

Live conditional forwarding on the office line that sends closed-hours and carrier-failure calls to the agent, leaves every human-answered call alone, and reverts to today the moment the rules come off.

Done when

A test call inside the closed window reaches the agent, a test call during staffed hours is answered by a person and never touched, and pulling the rules restores the phone to today's exact behavior, all three verified live.

Your off-ramp You can stop after seeing the routing tested and keep the backstop on the shelf. Removing the forwarding rules returns the line to today in minutes, with no lock-in. You keep your phone exactly as it was, plus a documented, ready-to-activate forwarding configuration you can switch on any later season with one message.

Done when

Your off-ramp You can stop after the pilot and keep the after-hours backstop running exactly as piloted, without ever expanding into the lunch hour or overflow. After-hours alone is a complete, ownable product. You keep a live, proven after-hours and carrier-failure backstop on your own line, the daytime phone entirely untouched, plus every transcript and note from the pilot.

Done when

Your off-ramp You can stop the expansion at either sub-step, keeping after-hours plus lunch but declining overflow, or after-hours alone. Each window is switched on only after its own readiness check passes, and any can be pulled without affecting the others. You keep whichever windows you approved, running live and independently, with the daytime human answer always taking precedence over the agent.

If in doubt the window stays off and the other two run without it.

The whole engagement, honestly

End to end, from kickoff to a system you fully own, this runs about four to eight weeks. The fast end assumes prompt sign-offs and a cooperative carrier. The slow end is driven almost entirely by peak-season review turnaround and the carrier's forwarding queue, not by our engineering. Our actual hands-on build time inside that span is under one week, about six to eight working days. The rest is your sign-off, carrier provisioning, and evidence-driven pilot windows.

The single biggest schedule risk

It lives on your side, not ours, and we would rather say so plainly than pretend otherwise. Your scarce peak-season review time on the two critical-path content sign-offs, the fact base and the greeting, is the thing most likely to stretch the calendar. The third-party carrier's conditional-forwarding provisioning is a close second. Both are client-side dependencies. Neither is our build. That is the honest shape of this. Our engineering is fast, and the calendar is your sign-off and the carrier. We have pre-drafted every default so that even a silent, slammed peak season still ships a safe launch.
